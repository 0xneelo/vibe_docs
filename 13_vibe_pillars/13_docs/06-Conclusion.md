# Conclusion

Vibe's architecture can be summarized through three pillars:

1. It must be hard to exploit.
2. It must make markets tradable before continuous two-sided flow exists.
3. It must generate enough yield for the parties supplying risk capital.

Each pillar is necessary. None is sufficient on its own.

This is what makes permissionless perpetuals for low-cap assets a distinct market design problem rather than a simple extension of existing perp venues. Mature markets can lean on synchronous matching and deep liquidity. Early markets cannot. They need a structure that can safely intermediate risk, bootstrap counterparties, and do so with enough capital efficiency to remain attractive.

That is the real meaning of the Vibe thesis. The protocol is not defined only by what it lists. It is defined by the architectural constraints it solves. If those three pillars hold, the long tail becomes tradable. If they fail, permissionless perpetuals remain stuck serving only the assets that were already liquid enough to succeed without them.
