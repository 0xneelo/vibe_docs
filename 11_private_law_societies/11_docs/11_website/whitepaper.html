The Symmio Symmio Sovereign Intent Protocol (SSIP)

A Symmetrical Private Law Society: Fusing Anarcho-Capitalism, Bilateral Settlement, and Prediction Market Governance

Version 1.0 | January 2026

Chapter 1: Executive Summary

1.1 Abstract

This whitepaper presents The Symmio Symmio Sovereign Intent Protocol (SSIP)—a radical restructuring of societal governance that replaces the nation-state model with an Automated Market for Governance hosted within autonomous zones. The protocol synthesizes three foundational frameworks:

  1. Oliver Janich's Private Law Society (Privatrechtsordnung/PRG): The philosophical foundation establishing that all legitimate social order emerges from voluntary contracts bound by the Non-Aggression Principle (NAP).
  2. The SYMMIO Protocol: The technical settlement infrastructure providing bilateral isolation, symmetrical contracts, collateral management (CVA), and solver competition mechanisms.
  3. Futarchy (Robin Hanson): The governance validation layer replacing democratic voting with prediction markets that bet on outcomes rather than intentions.

The result is a system where:

  • Laws become n-dimensional derivatives protecting citizens against rights violations
  • The State is recognized as an insolvent counterparty that cannot be liquidated
  • Justice is automated through smart contract execution
  • Security becomes a market commodity traded on efficiency, not political favor

1.2 The Problem Statement

Current governance models suffer from what libertarian economists term the "Monopoly Problem":

"The state is an institution that possesses a monopoly on the legitimate use of force within a given territory—yet bears no liability for its failures."

— Adapted from Max Weber, critiqued by Oliver Janich

Key Inefficiencies Identified:

  1. The Security Paradox: Modern states spend approximately 100x more resources on processing illegal immigrants than on protecting tax-paying citizens.
  2. The Taxation Fallacy: Mandatory taxation constitutes wealth extraction under threat of violence.
  3. The Jurisdiction Monopoly: Citizens cannot "fire" bad judges or "unsubscribe" from incompetent law enforcement.
  4. The Principal-Agent Problem: Elected officials face no personal liability for policy failures.

1.3 The Solution: Intent-Based Governance

The Symmio Sovereign Intent Protocol transforms governance from a monopolistic imposition into a market service:

Traditional State Symmio Sovereign Intent Protocol
Taxes (mandatory extraction) Premiums (voluntary insurance)
Laws (top-down imposition) Intents (bottom-up preferences)
Police (monopoly force) Private Security Agencies (competing PSAs)
Courts (state-appointed) Private Courts & Arbiters (competing judges)
Democracy (voting on people) Futarchy (betting on outcomes)
Punishment (incarceration) Restitution (CVA liquidation)

1.4 Key Definitions

Symmio Symmio Sovereign Intent Protocol (SSIP): A decentralized governance framework where citizens broadcast their preferences ("Intents") for protection, dispute resolution, and social order, which are then fulfilled by competing providers ("Solvers") through bilateral, collateral-backed contracts.

Private Security Agencies (PSAs): Private firms that provide physical protection, patrol, emergency response, and enforcement services in competition with each other.

Private Courts / Arbiters: Independent judges and arbitration services that compete to provide dispute resolution. Judges stake their own capital and lose it if they deliver corrupt or incompetent rulings.

Symmetrical Contract: A bilateral agreement where both parties lock collateral, ensuring mutual accountability.

Credit Valuation Adjustment (CVA): Locked collateral used to guarantee performance. In SSIP, CVA functions as "social security"—automatically distributed to clients if a provider becomes insolvent.

Chapter 2: Introduction — The Symmio Sovereign Intent Protocol Defined

2.1 What is the Symmio Sovereign Intent Protocol?

The Symmio Symmio Sovereign Intent Protocol (SSIP) represents the first comprehensive attempt to encode Oliver Janich's theoretical "Private Law Society" (Privatrechtsgesellschaft or PRG) into executable smart contract logic using the infrastructure pioneered by the SYMMIO Protocol.

At its core, SIP answers a fundamental question:

How do we transition from theoretical anarcho-capitalism to a functioning, implementable system that can compete with—and ultimately replace—the nation-state?

The answer lies in recognizing that the tools already exist. The SYMMIO Protocol provides all the necessary primitives:

  1. Intent Broadcasting: Users declare what they want
  2. Solver Competition: Providers compete to fulfill intents
  3. Bilateral Isolation: Each contract is independent
  4. Collateral Management: CVA ensures accountability
  5. Arbiter Verification: Third parties validate real-world outcomes

2.2 Why the State is an "Insolvent Counterparty"

In SYMMIO's framework, a counterparty is considered solvent when they maintain sufficient collateral to honor their obligations. The State fails this test catastrophically.

The citizen's obligations are enforced with lethal force (try not paying taxes). The state's obligations carry no enforcement mechanism whatsoever.

When a private company fails to deliver services, customers leave. When the state fails, it simply raises taxes or prints money. In SYMMIO terms, the state is a counterparty that cannot be liquidated, making it the ultimate "bad debt" in the governance market.

Chapter 4: The NAP as Root Code

"No man has a natural right to commit aggression on the equal rights of another, and this is all from which the laws ought to restrain him."

— Thomas Jefferson

4.1 The Non-Aggression Principle

NAP Statement: No individual may initiate force or fraud against another individual's person or property.

In SSIP, the NAP is not merely a guiding principle—it is encoded into the protocol's smart contracts as an inviolable constraint.

contract SolverRegistry {
    uint256 public constant NAP_BOND = 1_000_000 * 1e18;
    
    function registerSolver(bytes calldata napCommitment) external payable {
        require(msg.value >= NAP_BOND, "Insufficient NAP bond");
        require(verifyNAPCommitment(napCommitment), "Invalid NAP commitment");
        // ...
    }
    
    function reportNAPViolation(
        address solver,
        bytes calldata evidence,
        bytes[] calldata arbiterSignatures
    ) external {
        // Progressive slashing based on violation severity
        uint256 slashAmount = calculateSlash(evidence);
        solvers[solver].napBond -= slashAmount;
        
        // Permanent ban after 3 violations
        if (solvers[solver].violationCount >= 3) {
            blacklistSolver(solver);
        }
    }
}

Chapter 5: Symmetrical Jurisprudence

5.1 Private Contracts Replace Public Law

In the current system, laws are imposed by legislatures the individual did not elect. In the Private Law Society:

  • Every legal obligation stems from a voluntary contract
  • Every dispute is resolved by mutually pre-approved arbiters
  • Every enforcement action is a breach remedy, not state violence

5.2 Bilateral Isolation

SYMMIO's bilateral isolation principle ensures that the failure of any specific citizen-provider relationship does not affect any other relationship. There is no "Too Big to Fail" in bilateral governance.
