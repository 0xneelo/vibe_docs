01 — Introduction

Reimagining Governance

The Symmio Sovereign Intent Protocol (SSIP) represents the first comprehensive attempt to encode Oliver Janich's theoretical "Private Law Society" into executable smart contract logic using the infrastructure pioneered by the SYMMIO Protocol.

Current governance models suffer from what libertarian economists term the "Monopoly Problem": the state possesses a monopoly on the legitimate use of force within a given territory—yet bears no liability for its failures.

"The state is an insolvent counterparty that cannot be liquidated."

— Sovereign Intent Protocol, Chapter 2

03 — Mechanism

How It Works

1

Citizen Broadcasts Intent

A citizen declares their preferences for protection, dispute resolution, and social order through the protocol.

Intent: "I need property protection for 123 Liberty St."
2

Solvers Compete

Private Security Agencies and Private Courts compete to fulfill the intent, offering quotes with their collateral.

PSA Alpha: "5 USDC/month, 10,000 CVA locked"
3

Bilateral Contract Formed

Both parties lock collateral in a symmetrical smart contract, ensuring mutual accountability.

Contract: Citizen ⟷ PSA Alpha [Bilateral Isolation]
4

Performance Validated

Arbiter oracles verify service delivery. Prediction markets bet on provider quality. CVA is released or slashed.

Result: Service delivered ✓ → CVA released to PSA
