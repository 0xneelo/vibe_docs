# Conclusion

The game theory of listings explains why so many crypto outcomes feel distorted without requiring bad actors to explain them.

If a venue bears cost, reputation, or gatekeeping responsibility, it must list according to perceived interest rather than actual realized interest. That pushes the market toward prediction, curation, and interference. Projects learn to optimize for appearance. Venues learn to avoid embarrassment. The market becomes safer in one dimension, but less evolutionary in another.

Zero-cost permissionless systems make the opposite trade-off. They tolerate more noise and more failure in exchange for preserving genuine discovery. That is why they remain the birthplace of so many unexpected winners.

The strategic importance of Vibe follows from this directly. If crypto wants a genuinely permissionless derivative layer, it cannot wait until tokens are already obvious, validated, and institutionally legible. It must bring perpetuals into the zero perceived interest zone where future demand is still hidden.

That is the real thesis of the paper: **listing design is not just an exchange policy. It is a theory of market evolution.**
