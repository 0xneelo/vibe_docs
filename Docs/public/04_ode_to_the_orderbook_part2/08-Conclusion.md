# Section 8: Conclusion

Part I praised order books. Part II explains how to save them from the burden of premature selection.

The problem is not that order books fail at execution. They fail at being forced to guess which markets deserve execution before the market has matured. That is why listing becomes vibes-based, and why the industry keeps reproducing gatekeeping in new forms.

Vibe solves this by creating a permissionless perpetual layer that reveals real demand before the order book must commit. It turns listing from a political question into a market question.

That is the deeper sequel thesis:

- order books are the final layer
- Vibe is the proving ground beneath them
- the future of listings is automatic graduation, not manual selection

The end of vibes-based listings is what makes the house of all finance possible.
