# CHAPTER 16: THE TRANSITION STRATEGY

## 16.1 Implementation in Autonomous Zones

The SIP is designed for deployment in jurisdictionally autonomous zones:

**16.1.1 Target Zones**

| Zone | Jurisdiction | Status | SIP Readiness |
|------|--------------|--------|---------------|
| Próspera | Honduras ZEDE | Operational | High |
| Catawba Digital Economic Zone | USA (Tribal) | Developing | Medium |
| Free Private Cities (Various) | Multiple | Conceptual | Variable |
| Seasteading Zones | International Waters | Experimental | Medium |

**16.1.2 Próspera Implementation Roadmap**

```
PHASE 1: Foundation (Year 1)
├── Deploy core smart contracts on EVM chain
├── Establish initial PJA partnerships
├── Launch citizen registration system
├── Create basic intent marketplace
└── Initialize Futarchy governance markets

PHASE 2: Expansion (Year 2)
├── Onboard multiple competing PJAs
├── Deploy AI legislative systems
├── Launch defense derivatives market
├── Enable full restitution automation
└── Integrate IoT protection systems

PHASE 3: Maturation (Year 3+)
├── Achieve >50% resident participation
├── Full inter-PJA resolution protocols
├── Defense capability demonstration
├── Begin replication to new zones
└── Publish open-source reference implementation
```

## 16.2 Legal Integration Strategy

**16.2.1 Dual-Track Compliance**

SIP operates on two legal tracks simultaneously:

1. **Host Jurisdiction Track**: Maintain minimum compliance with host territory laws
2. **SIP Native Track**: Internal governance via intent-based bilateral contracts

**16.2.2 Arbitration Recognition**

Ensure SIP arbitration decisions are enforceable under:
- New York Convention (international arbitration)
- Host jurisdiction arbitration laws
- Bilateral investment treaties
