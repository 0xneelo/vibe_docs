# Section 9: Conclusion

The future is not RFQ versus order book. It is RFQ before order book.

Vibe improves order book DEXs by solving their upstream problem. It bootstraps markets that are too early, too small, or too uncertain for a full order book. It measures real demand, enables shorting, hardens token lifecycles, and creates objective graduation signals. Order books then inherit the survivors.

But the deeper strategic point is stronger still: Vibe does not need to compete with Hyperliquid first. It can become valuable by improving Hyperliquid first.

If Vibe becomes the system that helps the Hyperliquid community discover, test, and graduate new markets, then Vibe becomes difficult to replace. Trust begins to accumulate around the bootstrap layer itself. That creates a much stronger long-term position than trying to launch as a weaker copy of the endpoint.

This is the full claim of the paper:

- permissionless creation without permissionless derivatives is incomplete
- order books without a bootstrap layer remain selective gatekeepers
- a complete financial system needs both early discovery and mature execution
- the best way to challenge a dominant order book may be to first become indispensable to it
- SSHE can serve as a middle layer when direct Hyperliquid graduation is too expensive or premature

Vibe provides the first layer. Hyperliquid can provide the mature endpoint. SSHE can bridge the middle where needed. Together they form a continuous market lifecycle with real strategic optionality.

That is the ode: not to replace the order book immediately, but to become so useful to its ecosystem that replacement becomes optional rather than necessary.
